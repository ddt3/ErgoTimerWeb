## Welcome to ErgoTimer

ErgoTimer helps you plan a balanced working day by automatically scheduling mental tasks, physical breaks, and rest periods. This guide walks you through everything you need to do to get your first schedule up and running.

---

## Step 1: Install ErgoTimer

Download and install ErgoTimer from the Google Play Store. When you open the app for the first time, you may be asked to grant the following permissions:

- **Notifications** — required to display the current task and remaining time in your notification bar
- **Schedule Exact Alarm** — required to trigger task-completion alarms at the right time

Tap **Allow** when prompted. These permissions are necessary for the app to work correctly.

---

## Step 2: Open the Settings

When you first open ErgoTimer, you will see an empty schedule screen. Tap the **settings icon (⚙️)** in the top-right corner to open the Schedule Settings.

This is where you configure your personal working day. You only need to do this once — ErgoTimer remembers your settings.

---

## Step 3: Configure Your Working Day

Work through the settings sections:

### Core Schedule

| Setting | What it means | Recommended starting point |
|---|---|---|
| **Total Work Hours** | How many hours of mental work you plan to do today | Start low — e.g. 2–3 hours |
| **Max Mental Task Duration** | The longest you can focus before needing a break | Start with 30–60 minutes |
| **Min Physical Task Duration** | The minimum length of a physical break | 15 minutes is a good default |
| **Start Time** | What time your working day begins | Set this to when you typically start |

### Breaks & Recovery

- **Lunch Break**: Toggle this on if you take a lunch break. Set the start and end time of your lunch. ErgoTimer will automatically add a 30-minute rest period after lunch.
- **Bike Ride Before Work**: If you cycle before starting work, set the duration here. This will be added to the start of your schedule.
- **Bike Ride After Work**: If you cycle after work, set the duration here. This will be added at the end.

### Alerts

- **Sound Alarm on Task End**: Make sure this is turned on so you hear an alarm when a task finishes.
- **Alarm Ringtone**: Choose which sound plays at the end of a task.
- **Micro Pause Reminder**: If you want gentle reminders to take a short break during long mental tasks, set an interval here (e.g. every 30 minutes).

### Interface

- **Language**: Choose between English and Dutch.
- **Calm Interface Mode**: If you find the default interface too busy, turn this on for a simpler, less cluttered view.

---

## Step 4: Generate Your Schedule

Once you have entered your settings, tap the **Generate Schedule** button at the bottom of the settings panel.

ErgoTimer will create a personalised schedule for the day, with mental tasks, physical breaks, and rest periods automatically balanced based on your settings.

---

## Step 5: Review Your Schedule

Close the settings panel. You will now see your day laid out in a colour-coded schedule:

| Colour | Task type |
|---|---|
| 🟢 Green | Mental task |
| 🔴 Red | Physical break |
| 🔵 Blue | Rest break |
| 🟣 Purple | Lunch |

Each row shows the task name, start time, end time, and duration. Take a moment to look through the schedule. If something doesn't look right, go back to settings and adjust your configuration.

---

## Step 6: Start Your Day

When you are ready to begin, tap the **Start Day** button.

The first task in your schedule will begin immediately. At the top of the screen you will see:

- The name of the current task
- A countdown timer showing how much time is left
- A mental battery indicator showing your energy level
- Control buttons: **Pause**, **Stop**, and **Next**

---

## Step 7: Work Through Your Tasks

Follow the schedule as ErgoTimer guides you through the day:

- **When a task ends**, an alarm will sound and a notification will appear. Tap **OK** to move to the next task automatically.
- **If you need to pause**, tap the **Pause** button. Tap **Resume** to continue.
- **If you want to skip ahead**, tap the **Next** button. ErgoTimer will recalculate the remaining schedule to make sure you still reach your total work hours.
- **If you need to stop a task early**, tap the **Stop** button.

---

## Step 8: Adjust as You Go

You can adjust task durations directly in the schedule list while the day is running:

- Tap the **edit icon** on any task to reveal adjustment controls
- Use the **+10 min** and **−10 min** buttons to make the task longer or shorter
- For rest and physical breaks, you can switch between the two types using the toggle button

Any adjustments automatically recalculate the rest of the schedule so your total work hours are preserved.

---

## Tips for Getting the Most Out of ErgoTimer

- **Start with shorter tasks** — if you're new to structured scheduling, begin with a max mental task time of 30–45 minutes and adjust upward as you feel comfortable.
- **Honour the breaks** — the physical and rest breaks are not optional extras; they are a core part of managing your energy. Take them seriously.
- **Use micro-pause reminders** — during a long mental task, a 5-minute micro-pause (like making a coffee) can make a real difference.
- **Use Calm Mode** if you find the interface distracting or overwhelming.
- **Be consistent** — the more regularly you use ErgoTimer, the better you will become at estimating your own limits.

---

*Once your settings are saved, starting a new day is as simple as opening the app and tapping **Start Day**.*
